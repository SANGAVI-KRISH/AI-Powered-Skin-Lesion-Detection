About

Skin lesions refer to abnormal growths or changes in the skin. They can be harmless or indicate serious conditions such as melanoma. Early detection and diagnosis can significantly improve treatment outcomes.

General Info

A skin lesion is any abnormality on the skin, including rashes, moles, sores, or discoloration. Skin lesions have been studied for centuries, with early records indicating various natural remedies and surgical treatments.

Causes

Common causes include excessive sun exposure, genetic predisposition, viral infections, allergic reactions, and chronic diseases like diabetes or autoimmune disorders.

Treatment

Treatment options vary based on the severity of the lesion. Mild cases may be treated with topical creams, while serious cases like melanoma require surgical removal, chemotherapy, or radiation therapy.
